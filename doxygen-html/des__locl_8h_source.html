365  /* IP and FP
366  * The problem is more of a geometric problem that random bit fiddling.
367  0 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 62 54 46 38 30 22 14 6
368  8 9 10 11 12 13 14 15 60 52 44 36 28 20 12 4
369  16 17 18 19 20 21 22 23 58 50 42 34 26 18 10 2
370  24 25 26 27 28 29 30 31 to 56 48 40 32 24 16 8 0
371 
372  32 33 34 35 36 37 38 39 63 55 47 39 31 23 15 7
373  40 41 42 43 44 45 46 47 61 53 45 37 29 21 13 5
374  48 49 50 51 52 53 54 55 59 51 43 35 27 19 11 3
375  56 57 58 59 60 61 62 63 57 49 41 33 25 17 9 1
376 
377  The output has been subject to swaps of the form
378  0 1 -> 3 1 but the odd and even bits have been put into
379  2 3 2 0
380  different words. The main trick is to remember that
381  t=((l>>size)^r)&(mask);
382  r^=t;
383  l^=(t<<size);
384  can be used to swap and move bits between words.
385 
386  So l = 0 1 2 3 r = 16 17 18 19
387  4 5 6 7 20 21 22 23
388  8 9 10 11 24 25 26 27
389  12 13 14 15 28 29 30 31
390  becomes (for size == 2 and mask == 0x3333)
391  t = 2^16 3^17 -- -- l = 0 1 16 17 r = 2 3 18 19
392  6^20 7^21 -- -- 4 5 20 21 6 7 22 23
393  10^24 11^25 -- -- 8 9 24 25 10 11 24 25
394  14^28 15^29 -- -- 12 13 28 29 14 15 28 29
395 
396  Thanks for hints from Richard Outerbridge - he told me IP&FP
397  could be done in 15 xor, 10 shifts and 5 ands.
398  When I finally started to think of the problem in 2D
399  I first got ~42 operations without xors. When I remembered
400  how to use xors :-) I got it to its final state.
401  */
402 #define PERM_OP(a,b,t,n,m) ((t)=((((a)>>(n))^(b))&(m)),\
403  (b)^=(t),\
404  (a)^=((t)<<(n)))
405

411  PERM_OP(r,l,tt, 2,0x33333333L); \
412  PERM_OP(l,r,tt, 8,0x00ff00ffL); \
